SURFACE TEMPERATURE DYNAMICS OF HALDIA MUNICIPALITY

LAND SURFACE TEMPERATURE

Analyzed the Land Surface Temperature (LST) changes in Haldia Municipality over a 30-year period using Landsat Archives from 1991, 2001, 2011, and 2021.

This comparative study of Land Surface Temperature (LST) for Haldia Municipality reveals a clear and alarming trend of increasing surface temperature, pointing to rapid urbanization and loss of vegetation.

This study analyzes the Land Surface Temperature (LST) change in Haldia Municipality from 1991 to 2021 using satellite data. Over the 30-year period, the LST increased significantly from a range of 21°C - 30°C in 1991 to 25°C - 40°C in 2021.

This analysis underscores the need for urban greening, better land-use planning, and climate-resilient infrastructure to combat rising surface temperatures.

Urban Thermal Field Variance Index

Based on the provided UTFVI maps for Haldia from 1991 to 2021, a clear trend of intensifying urban heat island effects is evident over the three-decade period. The index, which assesses the ecological evaluation of land surface temperature, shows a significant expansion of areas falling into the higher categories (0.015–0.025 and >0.025).

This progression indicates that zones with the strongest thermal discomfort and negative environmental impact have grown substantially, likely due to extensive urbanization, industrial activity, and loss of green space. Consequently, the extent of land with the most favorable (lowest) UTFVI values has noticeably diminished.

This spatial analysis demonstrates a pronounced deterioration in Haldia's urban thermal environment, pointing towards increased heat stress for residents and a greater environmental burden on the local ecosystem.

Ecological Discomfort Zone

The maps depict the spatio-temporal changes in ecologically discomfort zones of Haldia Municipality between 2001 and 2021. These zones, characterized by high surface temperatures (above 30°C) and sparse vegetation cover, have shown a remarkable increase over the two decades.

In 2001, the discomfort areas were limited and scattered, mainly in the central part. By 2011, these zones expanded noticeably, indicating the growing impact of industrialization and urbanization. In 2021, the entire municipality exhibited widespread ecological discomfort, covering almost all regions except a few along the riverbanks.

The standard deviational ellipse analysis reveals a northeastward shift and an increase in the spatial spread of these zones, suggesting that thermal stress and environmental degradation are intensifying over time. This pattern reflects the combined influence of rapid urban expansion, loss of green spaces, and industrial activities affecting local ecological balance.
